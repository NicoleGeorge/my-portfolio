fashion or: buckle it up ... pt 1

May 11, 201y by Nicole

Ok so, I have decided to fuse the two loves of my life, fashion and my incessant love of research (yes...yes I am a lil' bit of a nerd). Not too sure where that came from, an insatiable curiosity, perhaps stemming from my Sagittarius need to figure out the world and how it works. Albeit to some they may come across as polar opposites, but as ‘They’ say, opposites attract. I will endeavor to share with you my take on fashion, from a 'fashademic' perspective if you will, from the self identity concepts that underpin style blogging, to why some trends fly and some fall whilst others just keep popping up every couple of decades...and everything in between.

In my first foray, I have delved into the seemingly fabulous world of fashion public relations. I acknowledge that there may be a few misconceptions about the industry, where in an attempt to dispel these falsities surrounding the fashion PR industry, I hope to shine a little light on how Fashion and PR seamlessly come together...enjoy.

What is Fashion PR you may ask? …or may not.Buckle it up!

The fashion PR industry has come to the fore recently with names like Kelly Cutrone of American fashion PR firm People’s Revolution and Sydney-based Sweaty Betty PR’s Chief Betty Roxy Jacenko becoming just as well known in their own right than the clients in which they represent.

Ok so…what exactly is Fashion PR? Buckle it up!

The fashion’s PR role at an organisational level can often be largely unnoticed by the general public. A fashion PR’s work must be continuous, carefully organised throughout the fashion year and they must have a well-rounded understanding and experience within the fashion industry at large from manufacturing, to wholesale and at the retail level (Constantino, 1998). Fashion PR is often renowned for being one of the most glamorous and high profile forms of PR.

This romanticised superficiality, however, has been perpetuated through reality television in shows like The Hills and The City it can be said…that just because the public doesn’t fully understand public relations, doesn’t mean that it should be reduced to a cultural cliché of endless parties and red carpets. This assertion begs the question…is fashion public relations merely rhetorical fluff spun by archetypal Samantha Jones characters?…or, is it a core management function that establishes and maintains mutually beneficial relationships between an organisation and its publics on whom success and failure depend?
